What is 100% Orange Juice?

"100% Orange Juice" is a digital board game where up to four players (human or AI) choose characters from other Orange_Juice titles and compete to achieve "Normas" (victory conditions) by collecting stars, winning battles, and utilizing cards. It combines elements of traditional board games with unique character abilities and card play.

Goal of the Game (Normas)

The ultimate goal is to complete 5 Norma objectives and then reach a home panel. Each Norma has a specific requirement, and you can choose between two types of objectives as you progress:

  • Star Normas: Focus on accumulating a certain number of stars.
  • Win Normas: Focus on defeating a certain number of enemies (NPCs or other players).

You progress through Normas by landing on your own Home Panel (or an opponent's Home Panel for Norma completion).

Here are the typical Norma requirements:

  • Norma 1 -> Norma 2: Get 10 stars.
  • Norma 2 -> Norma 3: Get 30 stars OR defeat 2 enemies.
  • Norma 3 -> Norma 4: Get 70 stars OR defeat 5 enemies.
  • Norma 4 -> Norma 5: Get 120 stars OR defeat 9 enemies.
  • Final Norma (after Norma 5): Return to a home panel after completing Norma 5.
Game Flow (Player Turn)

A turn in 100% Orange Juice generally follows these steps:

  • Field Events: Any ongoing field events (game-wide effects) take place.
  • Star Gain: All players gain stars equal to their current level. This amount increases every few turns.
  • player's turn:
    • KO Check: If the player is KO'd, they roll to revive. If successful, they skip to step 3e. If not, they skip to step 4.
    • Play Card: The player may play one card from their hand, if applicable.
    • Roll to Move: The player rolls a d6 (six-sided die) to determine how many spaces they move.
      • Movement Choice: Players can choose their path at forks.
      • Stopping on Home: Players can choose to stop on their own Home Panel even if their roll would take them past it. This is crucial for completing Normas.
      • Challenging Opponents: Players can choose to stop and challenge an opponent on a space along their path.
    • Trap Activation: If the player lands on a panel with a trap card, it activates.
    • Panel Effect: The panel the player stopped on activates its effect (e.g., Star Panel, Battle Panel, Draw Panel).
    • Discard Cards: If the player has more than their maximum hand size (usually 3) and is not KO'd, they must discard cards until they are at their maximum.
  • Next Player's Turn: The turn passes to the next player.
Key Game elements

Characters: Each character has unique stats (HP, ATK, DEF, EVD) and a "Hyper Card" (a powerful, character-specific ability). Characters also have passive abilities that influence their playstyle.

Stars: The primary currency and often the goal of the game. Stars are used to play cards and are gained through various means (level-up bonus, Star Panels, winning battles).

Cards: Players build a deck of 10 cards before the game starts. Cards have various effects:

  • Battle Cards: Used in combat to modify rolls or create special effects.
  • Event Cards: Affect the board or all players.
  • Trap Cards: Placed on panels to hinder opponents.
  • Gift Cards: Given to other players, often with negative effects.
  • Hyper Cards: Powerful, character-specific cards that are usually drawn directly by their associated character but can be used by others if acquired.

Panels: Different types of spaces on the board with specific effects when a player lands on them:

  • Home Panel: Your starting and Norma-completion point. Can be used to heal.
  • Star Panel: Grants stars based on a dice roll.
  • Battle Panel: Triggers a battle with a random enemy (or another player if they are on the space).
  • Draw Panel: Allows the player to draw cards.
  • Trap Panel: Activates a trap card placed on it.
  • Encounter Panel: Can trigger various events or encounters.

Combat: When a player initiates a battle or lands on a Battle Panel:

  • Attack Roll: The attacker rolls a die, modified by their ATK stat.
  • Defense/Evade: The defender chooses to either Defend (roll a die, modified by DEF, to reduce damage, minimum 1 damage) or Evade (roll a die, modified by EVD, to potentially nullify all damage if the evade roll is higher than the attack roll).
  • Damage: Calculated based on the attack and defense/evade rolls.
  • KO: If a player's HP drops to 0, they are KO'd and lose half their stars. They must then roll to revive on subsequent turns.
  • Wins: Defeating enemies grants "wins," which are crucial for "Win Norma" objectives. NPCs give 1 win, other players give 2 wins, and bosses give 3 wins.

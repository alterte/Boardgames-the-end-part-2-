checkers manual

1. objective

The objective of Checkers is to capture all of your opponent’s pieces or block them so they cannot make a move.

2. Equipment

  • A standard 8×8 checkerboard (64 squares)
  • 12 pieces per player (usually black and red)

    • 3. Game Setup

    • The board is positioned so that each player has a dark square on the bottom-left corner.
    • Each player places their 12 pieces on the dark squares of the first three rows on their side.
    • The center two rows (rows 4 and 5) remain empty at the start.

    4. Basic Rules

    4.1 Movement

    • Regular pieces (called “men”) move diagonally forward one square.
    • A player can only move on dark squares.

    4.2 Capturing

    • If an opponent’s piece is diagonally adjacent and the square immediately beyond it (in the same direction) is empty, the player must jump over and capture the opponent’s piece.
    • Multiple captures in one turn (called a “double jump” or “multi-jump”) are allowed and must be completed if possible.
    • Captured pieces are removed from the board immediately.

    4.3 King Pieces

    • When a piece reaches the last row (the opponent’s first row), it becomes a King.
    • Kings are usually marked by stacking two pieces or flipping the piece.
    • Kings can move and capture both forward and backward diagonally.

    4.4 Mandatory Captures

    • If a capture is available, the player must make the capture.
    • If more than one capture is possible, the player can choose which sequence to follow.

    5. Winning The Game

    you win the game by:

    • Capturing all of your opponent’s pieces.
    • Blocking all of your opponent’s legal moves (stalemating them).

    6. Drawn game

    The game is drawn if:

    • Neither player can force a win.
    • A move limit is reached (often 40 moves without a capture or king movement).
    • Both players agree to a draw.

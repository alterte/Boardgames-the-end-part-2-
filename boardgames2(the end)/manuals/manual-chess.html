chess manual

Objective

The goal of chess is to checkmate your opponent’s king. Checkmate happens when the king is in a position to be captured (“in check”) and cannot escape from capture.

Game Setup

  • Chess is played on an 8x8 board with alternating light and dark squares.
  • Each player begins with 16 pieces:
    • 1 king
    • 1 queen
    • 2 rooks
    • 2 bishops
    • 2 knights
    • 8 pawns

      • white moves first, then players alternate turns

How The Pieces Move

♔ King
  • Moves one square in any direction.Moves one square in any direction.
  • Cannot move into check (a square attacked by an enemy piece).Cannot move into check (a square attacked by an enemy piece).
♕ Queen
  • Moves any number of squares vertically, horizontally, or diagonally.
♖ Rook
  • Moves any number of squares vertically or horizontally.
♗ Bishop
  • Moves any number of squares diagonally.
♘ Knight
  • Moves in an “L” shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular.
  • Can jump over other pieces.
♙ Pawn
  • Moves forward one square.
  • On its first move, it can move forward two squares.
  • Captures one square diagonally.
  • Cannot move backward.

Special Rules

Castling

  • A special move involving the king and a rook.

conditions:

  • Neither piece has moved before.
  • No pieces between the king and rook.
  • The king is not in check, does not pass through check, or end in check.
  • The king moves two squares toward the rook, and the rook jumps over it.

‣En passant

  • When a pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position and lands next to an opponent’s pawn.
  • The opponent may capture it as if it had moved only one square.
  • This must be done immediately on the next move.

‣Pawn Promotion

  • When a pawn reaches the opponent’s back rank (8th rank), it must be promoted to a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
  • Most often, pawns are promoted to queens.

check & checkmate

‣Check:

  • A king is in check if it is under threat of capture.
  • The player must make a move to get out of check

‣checkmate

  • The king is in check and cannot escape.
  • This ends the game with a win for the attacking player.

Draw (Tie Games)

A game can end in a draw in several ways:

  • Stalemate: The player has no legal move and is not in check.
  • Threefold Repetition: The same position occurs three times.
  • Fifty-Move Rule: No pawn move or capture has occurred in 50 consecutive moves.
  • Mutual Agreement: Both players agree to a draw.

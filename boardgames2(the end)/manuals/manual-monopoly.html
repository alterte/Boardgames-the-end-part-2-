monopoly game manual

the monopoly game manual objective

The object of the game is to become the wealthiest player through buying, renting, and selling property.

equipment

The equipment consists of a game board, 2 dice, tokens (playing pieces), 32 houses, 12 hotels, Chance cards, Community Chest cards, Title Deed cards for each property, and MONOPOLY game money.

preparation

  • Place the game board on a table.
  • Separate the Chance and Community Chest cards, shuffle each deck, and place them face-down on their designated spaces on the board.
  • Each player chooses a unique token to represent them on the board and places it on the "GO" space.
  • One player is selected to be the Banker. The Banker manages the Bank's assets: dealing out Title Deeds, money, and buildings to players, collecting taxes and penalty money, and conducting auctions. The Banker may also play, but must keep their personal money and properties separate from the Bank's.
  • The Banker gives each player $1500, divided as follows (this distribution may vary slightly by edition, but this is common):
    • Two $500 bills
    • Two $100 bills
    • Two $50 bills
    • Six $20 bills
    • Five $10 bills
    • Five $5 bills
    • Five $1 bills
  • All remaining money and other equipment (houses, hotels, Title Deeds not yet owned) go to the Bank.

The play

Starting the Game
Each player rolls both dice. The player with the highest total starts the play. In case of a tie, those players roll again. Play proceeds to the left (clockwise).
On Your Turn

  • Roll the Dice: Roll both dice and move your token clockwise around the board the number of spaces indicated by the sum of the dice.
  • Land on a Space and Take Action:
    • Unowned Property (Street, Railroad, Utility):
      • You may buy the property from the Bank for the price printed on the board space. Pay the Bank, and the Banker will give you the Title Deed card for that property. Place it face-up in front of you.
      • If you choose not to buy it at the printed price, the Banker immediately auctions the property to the highest bidder. Any player, including the one who declined to buy it, may bid. Bidding starts at any price. The highest bidder pays the Bank and receives the Title Deed.
    • owned property:
      • If you land on a property owned by another player, you must pay rent to that player. The amount of rent is stated on the Title Deed card and varies depending on whether the property is unimproved, part of a complete color group, or has houses or hotels.
      • If the property is mortgaged, no rent is collected.
      • The owner must ask for rent before the next player rolls the dice. If they forget to ask, you do not have to pay.
    • Chance or Community Chest:
      • Draw the top card from the respective deck, follow the instructions on the card, and then return it face-down to the bottom of the deck.
      • If you draw a "GET OUT OF JAIL FREE" card, you may keep it until needed or sell it to another player at an agreed price.
    • chance or community chest:
      • Draw the top card from the respective deck, follow the instructions on the card, and then return it face-down to the bottom of the deck.
      • If you draw a "GET OUT OF JAIL FREE" card, you may keep it until needed or sell it to another player at an agreed price.
    • Tax Spaces (Income Tax, Luxury Tax):
      • You must pay the amount indicated on the board to the Bank.You must pay the amount indicated on the board to the Bank.
    • "GO" (Start):
      • When your token lands on or passes over "GO" (even if you land on another space due to a card), the Bank pays you a $200 salary.
    • jail:
      • you land in jail if:
        • Your token lands on the space marked "GO TO JAIL."
        • You draw a "GO TO JAIL" card from Chance or Community Chest.
        • You roll doubles three times in succession on your turn.
      • to get out of jail:
        • You can pay a $50 fine to the Bank before your turn to roll the dice.
        • You can use a "GET OUT OF JAIL FREE" card if you have one.
        • You can try to roll doubles on your next three turns. If you succeed, you immediately move the number of spaces indicated by the doubles and your turn ends (you do not get to roll again for rolling doubles in Jail).
        • If you fail to roll doubles on your third attempt, you must pay the $50 fine, and then you move the number of spaces indicated by that third roll.
        • While in Jail, you can still collect rent on your properties, buy and sell houses/hotels, and mortgage properties.
        • If you are "just visiting" Jail (you landed on the Jail space but were not sent there), you are simply visiting and move on your next turn as usual.
    • Free Parking:
      • Nothing happens when you land here. It's a "free" resting place. You do not collect any money.
  • doubles: If you roll doubles, after completing your action for the space you landed on, you get to roll the dice again and take another turn. If you roll doubles three times in a row, you must immediately "GO TO JAIL" (see above).

Buying Buildings (Houses and Hotels)

  • You can only buy houses and hotels when you own all the properties in a complete color group (a monopoly).
  • Houses must be built evenly on the properties within a color group. You cannot build a second house on any property in that group until you have built one house on every property in that group. Similarly, for a third, and then a fourth house.
  • The price of each house is printed on the Title Deed card.
  • Once you have four houses on every property in a complete color group, you can upgrade them to a hotel. You pay the hotel price (printed on the Title Deed) and return the four houses to the Bank. You can only have one hotel per property.
  • You can buy buildings on your turn or between other players' turns. The Bank has a limited supply of 32 houses and 12 hotels. If there are no buildings left in the Bank, players must wait for another player to sell their buildings back to the Bank.

Selling Property And Buildings

  • Buildings (Houses and Hotels): You can sell houses and hotels back to the Bank at any time for one-half of their original purchase price.
  • Houses must be sold evenly, in reverse of how they were built (e.g., if you have four houses on a property, you must sell one from each property in the group before selling a second from any property, and so on).
  • When selling a hotel, you receive half the cost of the hotel AND the four houses that were replaced by the hotel. The four houses are returned to the Bank.
  • Unimproved Properties: You can sell unimproved properties (those without houses or hotels) to any player through a private transaction for any agreed-upon price.
  • Properties cannot be sold to another player if there are any buildings on any property within that color group. All buildings on that color group must be sold back to the Bank first.
  • Mortgaged Properties: Mortgaged properties can be sold to other players at an agreed price. When buying a mortgaged property, the new owner must immediately either pay the Bank 10% interest on the mortgage amount (and the property remains mortgaged) or pay the Bank the full mortgage amount plus 10% interest to unmortgage it.

Mortagages

  • If you need money, you can mortgage unimproved properties to the Bank. The mortgage value is printed on the back of each Title Deed card.
  • When a property is mortgaged, its Title Deed card is turned face-down. No rent can be collected on a mortgaged property.
  • To unmortgage a property, you must pay the Bank the mortgage value plus 10% interest.
  • Houses and Hotels must be sold back to the Bank (at half their cost) before a property in that color group can be mortgaged. Bankruptcy
  • You are bankrupt if you owe more money than you can raise through selling buildings, mortgaging properties, or selling unimproved properties to other players.
  • If you owe the Bank, all your assets (cash, properties, Get Out of Jail Free cards) are turned over to the Bank. Any mortgaged properties are immediately put up for auction by the Banker.
  • If you owe another player, you must turn over all your assets to that player. If any properties are mortgaged, the new owner must immediately pay the Bank 10% interest on each mortgaged property. They then have the option to pay the full mortgage amount plus 10% to unmortgage it, or they can leave it mortgaged and unmortgage it later by paying the mortgage value plus 10% interest.
  • Once bankrupt, you are out of the game.

    • THE WINNER

    The last player remaining in the game is the winner.

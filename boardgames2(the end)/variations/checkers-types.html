checkers variations

Checkers isn’t a one-size-fits-all game! From American to Russian to International styles, each country adds its own spin with different boards, moves, and strategies. Dive in and discover how this classic game changes across the globe — you might just find a new favorite way to play.

Types of Checkers by Country:

🇺🇸🇬🇧American Checkers (English Draughts):
  • Board: 8x8
  • Rules: Only forward jumps, kings move diagonally forward/backward.
  • Most popular version in the U.S. and English-speaking countries.
🇫🇷French checkers (jeu de dames):
  • Board: 10x10
  • Kings: Can move and jump any number of squares diagonally (like in international checkers).
  • Capturing is mandatory and “maximum capture” rule applies.
International Draughts (Also called “Polish Draughts”):
  • Board: 10x10
  • Popular in: France, Netherlands, Russia, parts of Africa.
  • Kings: Long-range diagonal movement.
  • Capturing is mandatory with the most pieces taken if multiple options exist.
🇧🇷Brazilian checkers:
  • Board: 8x8
  • Same rules as International Draughts but on a smaller board.
  • Very popular in Brazil.
🇷🇺Russian Droughts
  • Board: 8x8
  • Unique rule: Pieces can jump both forward and backward even when not kinged.
  • Kinging happens after a move finishes (not mid-move).
  • Highly strategic, very popular in Russia and Eastern Europe.
🇪🇸 Spanish Checkers
  • Board: 8x8
  • Similar to Russian Checkers but no backward jumps for regular pieces.
  • Kings move only one square at a time.
🇮🇹 Italian Draughts
  • Board: 8x8
  • Very strict: no backward jumps, even for kings.
  • Captures must be made using normal pieces over kings if possible.
  • Tactical and defensive style.
